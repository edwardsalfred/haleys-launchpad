# "Why I Built This" — video script

**Length:** 60–90 seconds. **Setting:** somewhere comfortable and familiar —
your porch, the kitchen table, Haley's room. Good natural light near a window
beats a dark room with fancy lighting. Phone camera is fine; just prop it up
steady (stack of books, a mug, whatever works) and check the audio isn't
muffled.

Read this as a starting point, then say it in your own words — the goal is
that it sounds like *you*, not a script. Swap in real details about Haley
wherever you see `[ ]`.

---

**[0:00–0:10] Cold open — the hook**
Look at the camera like you're talking to a friend, not an audience.

> "Hi, I'm [your name] — and this is a course I built for my granddaughter,
> Haley."

**[0:10–0:30] The problem**
> "Haley's heading into 3rd grade this fall, and I wanted to help her get
> ready over the summer. But I didn't want to hand her another workbook, and
> I didn't want it to turn into a fight about screen time either.
> [Optional real detail: e.g. "She's more of a [reader / math kid / builder]
> than a sit-still-and-drill kind of kid."]"

**[0:30–0:55] What you built and why**
> "So I built her a course — real Mississippi 3rd grade Math and Reading
> standards, turned into a space adventure. She's a cadet flying missions,
> earning badges and stars, and she has to actually get the answers right to
> unlock the next one. She picks it up on her mom's phone whenever she wants,
> and I can check her progress without hovering over her shoulder."
> [Optional: cut to 3-5 seconds of Haley actually using the app on a phone —
> the dashboard, a lesson screen, or the confetti moment after passing a quiz.]

**[0:55–1:15] Why you're sharing it**
> "It worked so well for her that I didn't want to keep it just for our
> family. Every mission underneath the fun is still the real standard she
> needs for 3rd grade — that part never changed. If your kid is heading into
> 3rd grade this summer, I built this for you too."

**[1:15–1:25] Close / CTA**
> "It's free to start — just tap below. I hope it helps your cadet the way
> it's helped mine."

---

## B-roll ideas (optional, cut in during the "what you built" section)

- Haley's hands tapping through the dashboard or a lesson screen
- A close-up of a badge or confetti moment
- You and Haley looking at the phone together, smiling
- The planet/galaxy map screen scrolling by

## After recording

1. Export as MP4, drop it in as `video/haley-story.mp4` (see
   [`video/README.md`](../video/README.md) for format notes).
2. Optional: grab a nice still frame and save it as `video/poster.jpg` so the
   video has a thumbnail before anyone presses play.

   the file is there.
